# Character canon: Roan and Kiunin

This file updates the main character canon before more story patches are written.

## Cognitive numbers

### Roan

Roan's IQ is 150.

He is not just smart. He has a rare mutation called Cognitive Resistance, which lets him resist:

- memory erasure
- Sahur noise
- IQ drain
- false normality
- Brainrot mental influence

This does not make him automatically good at battles.

Roan still starts weak as a trainer because he avoided Brainrots for years. He understands patterns, lies, and hidden systems, but he lacks battle instinct.

### Normal residents of Kanto

Most residents now average around IQ 75 because Tung Tung Sahur God Form has been draining cognitive energy for years.

They are not evil or stupid by nature. Their minds have been damaged by Sahur noise.

This explains why people accept absurd things as normal.

Examples:

- people treat Brainrots like they always existed
- parents forget missing children
- workers trust Cognia too easily
- kids repeat Brainrot phrases without questioning them
- people forget details when someone talks against Sahur

## The Sahur Noise / Cognitive Lock

The Sahur Noise creates a mental lock.

This lock prevents most people from keeping information that threatens Tung Tung Sahur.

A person can hear the truth, understand it for a moment, and then lose it again.

This is why Roan has trouble convincing others.

The truth does not just sound unbelievable. The world itself tries to erase it from their minds.

## Rival name change

Old name:

`Caio`

New canon name:

`Kiunin`

Kiunin replaces Caio as Roan's main rival.

## Kiunin's personality

Kiunin starts as:

- extremely aggressive
- spoiled
- anxious
- arrogant
- loud
- competitive
- desperate for attention
- obsessed with status

He is not calm like a normal rival. He snaps quickly, talks over people, and treats battles like proof that he matters.

He is brave, but in a messy way. He rushes forward because standing still makes him panic.

## Kiunin's background

Kiunin is the son of an important Cognia researcher.

Because of this, he grew up protected, praised, and spoiled.

He always had better equipment, better tutors, and easier access to Brainrot training.

People acted like he was special before he proved anything.

This made him arrogant, but also deeply insecure.

He thinks if he stops winning, people will stop caring about him.

## Kiunin's early goal

At the start, Kiunin does not want to save the world.

He wants fame.

His dream is to become a Class S trainer because Class S trainers get:

- tournament invitations
- public fame
- money
- interviews
- status
- elite access

To him, Brainrots are a ladder.

He wants the title, the cameras, and the feeling of being above everyone else.

## Rivalry with Roan

Kiunin hates that Roan gets attention from Dr. Avelar despite not even liking Brainrots.

To Kiunin, this feels unfair.

He sees Roan as:

- cold
- strange
- ungrateful
- too serious
- bad at battles early on
- someone who wastes talent

Roan sees Kiunin as:

- loud
- spoiled
- dangerous
- too connected to Cognia
- someone who refuses to think deeply

Their rivalry starts toxic.

Kiunin wants to crush Roan to prove he is the real protagonist of the region.

Roan does not care about fame, which makes Kiunin even angrier.

## The long conversation arc

As the journey continues, Roan realizes Kiunin is not just arrogant.

Kiunin is trapped by Sahur noise.

Every time Roan explains the truth about Luna, Cognia, or Tung Tung, Kiunin almost understands.

Then the lock activates.

Kiunin forgets details, gets angry, changes the subject, or accuses Roan of lying.

Roan keeps trying.

For many days, Roan explains the same truth again and again.

Kiunin resists at first because accepting the truth means accepting that his whole life was built inside a lie.

## Breaking the lock

Breaking the Sahur lock in Kiunin should be almost impossible.

It happens slowly because of three things:

1. Roan's Cognitive Resistance keeps the truth stable near him.
2. Kiunin is emotionally tied to Roan through rivalry and trust.
3. Kiunin's own fear of being fake becomes stronger than the Sahur noise.

The break is not clean.

Kiunin does not suddenly become smart and calm.

He breaks down, panics, gets angry, and feels empty.

For the first time, he remembers the truth and cannot run from it.

## Kiunin's choice

After the lock weakens, Kiunin enters a mental gap.

He can choose between two lives:

### Option 1: the comfortable lie

He can live peacefully with Brainrots.

He can accept Cognia's story.

He can chase Class S fame, tournaments, money, and popularity.

He can pretend the world is normal.

### Option 2: the painful truth

He can accept that the world he trusted does not really exist.

He can accept that Cognia lied.

He can accept that Tung Tung is stealing minds.

He can help Roan save Kanto even if it destroys his own future.

Kiunin chooses the truth.

This is the moment he stops being only a rival and becomes Roan's real ally.

## Kiunin's growth

Kiunin's growth is not about becoming soft.

He stays intense, emotional, and competitive.

But he changes from selfish aggression into focused courage.

Early Kiunin says:

`I will be Class S before you even learn how to smile.`

Middle Kiunin says:

`I hate that you are right. I hate it. But I remember now.`

Late Kiunin says:

`If the world is fake, then I will tear the fake part down.`

Final Kiunin says:

`I am still standing. So we keep going.`

## Kiunin's father

Kiunin's father is an important Cognia researcher.

Suggested name:

`Dr. Renan Veyr`

Role:

- high-ranking Cognia scientist
- helped study Sahur noise
- hid parts of the truth
- loves Kiunin, but raised him inside privilege and lies
- believes controlling information is better than mass panic

Dr. Renan Veyr is not a pure villain.

He made terrible choices because he thought people could not handle the truth.

His death should hit Kiunin hard because Kiunin realizes his father was both a liar and someone who loved him.

## Strawberry Helefanto as a guard of Tung Tung

Strawberry Helefanto is changed from only a memory guardian into one of Tung Tung's great guards.

But there is a twist.

It was once tied to memory protection, but Tung Tung corrupted it.

Now Strawberry Helefanto guards stolen memories inside the Sahur system.

It does not just attack bodies. It attacks grief, nostalgia, and the desire to forget pain.

## Death scene: Kiunin's father

Near the late story, Roan and Kiunin battle alongside Dr. Renan Veyr against Strawberry Helefanto, one of Tung Tung's guards.

During the fight, Strawberry Helefanto uses stolen memories against them.

It shows Kiunin a fake life:

- his father proud of him
- Class S fame
- tournaments
- cameras
- a world where nothing is wrong

Kiunin almost falls into it.

Roan pulls him back by reminding him of the choice he already made.

Dr. Renan sacrifices himself to protect Kiunin and Roan from Strawberry Helefanto's memory attack.

He dies in front of Kiunin.

His last message is not a perfect apology.

It is broken and human:

`I thought lies could protect you. I was wrong. Live in the truth, Kiunin.`

Kiunin does not collapse like he would have at the start.

He shakes. He cries. He almost screams.

But he stays standing.

This is the proof that he changed.

He continues the journey with Roan to save the world from Tung Tung.

## Why this matters

At the start, Kiunin wants fame from the same system that hides the truth.

By the end, Kiunin loses the person who gave him access to that system.

Instead of running back into the lie, he chooses Roan, the truth, and the fight against Tung Tung.

His arc is:

`spoiled rival -> broken believer -> unstable ally -> truth-bearer -> warrior who keeps standing`

## Updated main duo dynamic

Roan is the mind that remembers.

Kiunin is the heart that refuses to fall after finally remembering.

Roan exposes the lie.

Kiunin proves a person born inside the lie can still choose the truth.
