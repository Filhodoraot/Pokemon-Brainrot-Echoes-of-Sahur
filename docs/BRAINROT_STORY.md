# Brainrot: Echoes of Sahur

## Core rule

Before the Sahur Incident, this world had no Pokemon and no Brainrots.

Kanto was a normal human region. The Brainrots appeared only after the first great cognitive rupture.

## Main characters

- Roan: protagonist. He remembers his sister Luna even after the world forgot her. He has IQ 150 and Cognitive Resistance, but starts weak as a trainer because he avoided Brainrots for years.
- Luna: Roan's sister. Kidnapped by Tung Tung Sahur God Form when she was 6. She has stronger Cognitive Resistance than Roan.
- Kiunin: Roan's rival. Aggressive, spoiled, loud, anxious, and obsessed with becoming famous as a Class S trainer. He slowly becomes Roan's ally after his Sahur mental lock starts breaking.
- Dr. Avelar: Brainrot researcher and leader of Training Center 01. He helps Roan enter the official license system.
- Dr. Renan Veyr: Kiunin's father. A high-ranking Cognia researcher who hid the truth to keep order. He loves Kiunin, but raised him inside lies.
- Dr. Dante Vilar: human villain. He serves Tung Tung God and steals cognitive energy from people.

## World state

Ten years after the Sahur Incident, Brainrots are treated as normal by most people.

But this is not truly normal. Since the incident, the average IQ of Kanto dropped to around 75. Most people do not question it because the power stealing their intelligence also weakens their ability to notice the problem.

Roan has Cognitive Resistance, a rare genetic mutation that lets some humans resist memory erasure, IQ drain, Sahur noise, and Brainrot mental influence.

Roan's high intelligence does not make battles easy. He avoided Brainrots for years, so he is weak as a trainer at the start. Other trainers understand Brainrot battles better because they grew up with them.

## Sahur Noise and Cognitive Lock

Sahur Noise is not just sound.

It is a mental pressure created by Tung Tung God Form. It can make people forget important facts, accept absurd things as normal, and avoid questions that would lead to the truth.

Some people almost understand the truth, but then their mind bends away from it.

This is called Cognitive Lock.

Kiunin is one of the most important victims of this lock.

## Prologue: The Sahur Incident

A child in Kanto screamed the phrase "Tung Tung Sahur".

At first, people thought it was nonsense. Then more children repeated it. Soon, children across Kanto were saying the same phrase without understanding why.

Doctors took the affected children to the Central Hospital of Saffron. Tests showed nothing wrong. The children were healthy, but they kept repeating the sound.

The doctors gathered all the children in one place to observe the condition.

That caused the first rupture.

From the shared desire, fear, sound, and repeated thought of the children, Tung Tung Sahur God Form appeared. It destroyed the hospital and kidnapped Luna, Roan's sister.

After the attack, the children returned to normal.

But everyone forgot Luna existed.

Only Roan remembered her.

## Saga 1: Training Center 01

At age 16, Roan joins Training Center 01. He does not want fame. He wants access to restricted areas so he can investigate Luna's disappearance.

Dr. Avelar gives Roan his first Brainrot. Roan hates the idea, but accepts because he needs a partner to survive.

Kiunin appears as Roan's rival.

Kiunin is the son of Dr. Renan Veyr, an important Cognia researcher. Because of that, he has better equipment, better access, and more confidence than Roan.

Kiunin wants to become a Class S trainer for fame, interviews, status, and proof that he matters.

He sees Roan as cold, strange, ungrateful, and weak in battle.

Roan sees Kiunin as spoiled, loud, dangerous, and too connected to Cognia.

Their rivalry starts toxic.

## Saga 2: License E

Roan's first test is to capture a wild Brainrot near the training zone.

This test proves if a candidate can stay mentally stable near wild Brainrots.

Kiunin passes easily because he is already comfortable with Brainrots.

Roan passes with more difficulty, but the researchers notice his Cognitive Resistance.

Kiunin gets angry because Dr. Avelar pays attention to Roan even though Roan barely likes Brainrots.

## Saga 3: Forest of the Field Demons

Roan enters the old Viridian Forest, now called Zone Green 01.

People once called the first wild Brainrots "field demons" because they appeared in forests after the incident.

Roan finds the Cult of the First Sound, a group that worships Tung Tung God.

The cult believes thinking too much is suffering and repetition is salvation.

Roan finds the first Sahur Fragment, proof that Luna was real.

Kiunin sees part of this proof, almost understands it, then forgets because of Cognitive Lock.

He accuses Roan of lying for attention.

This becomes the first major crack in their rivalry.

## Saga 4: Cognia Institute

Roan learns that the Cognia Institute has hidden the truth.

They know Kanto's average IQ dropped. They know memories were erased. They know Tung Tung God is not gone.

Dr. Renan Veyr, Kiunin's father, is revealed as one of the people who helped hide the truth.

Renan is not pure evil. He believes panic would destroy Kanto faster than the lie.

Kiunin refuses to believe his father could be involved.

Roan tries to explain Luna, Cognia, and Sahur Noise to Kiunin many times.

Each time, Kiunin almost gets it, then the lock pulls him away.

## Saga 5: License D

Roan takes a survival test. The test is sabotaged by the Grey Market, an organization that sells rare Brainrots and cognitive data.

Roan saves other candidates and passes.

Kiunin also helps, but pretends he only did it to protect his ranking.

For the first time, Kiunin shows that his aggression can become courage.

He still does not fully trust Roan.

## Saga 6: Cave 67

Roan enters the cave where a policeman was possessed years ago.

There he finds 67 Echo. 67 is not just a number. It is a cognitive key that opens paths between thought and reality.

67 does not evolve because it is a legendary Brainrot.

It may have a True 67 boss state later, but that is not normal evolution.

Kiunin enters the cave after Roan and gets trapped in a gesture loop.

Roan saves him.

Kiunin remembers the truth for a few seconds, then loses it again.

This scares him more than he admits.

## Saga 7: Dante Vilar

Dr. Dante Vilar is revealed as the human working with Tung Tung God.

Dante believes intelligence creates suffering. He wants to lower humanity's intelligence and create a world of peace through repetition.

He steals cognitive energy from people to feed Tung Tung God.

Dante notices Roan's Cognitive Resistance and sees him as a threat.

He notices Kiunin too, not because Kiunin resists well, but because Kiunin keeps trying to break the lock through emotion.

## Saga 8: License C

Roan investigates disappearing children in Cerulean.

The children return calmer and more obedient, but less curious.

Roan discovers that the Cult of the First Sound uses small Sahur waves to steal IQ from children.

Kiunin sees a child repeat the same lock pattern that happens to him.

For the first time, Kiunin asks Roan to explain the truth again.

He hates hearing it, but he asks.

## Saga 9: License Tests

The old gym system becomes Brainrot License Tests.

- License E: capture
- License D: survival
- License C: investigation
- License B: official battle
- License A: forbidden-zone exploration
- License S: access to the deepest secrets of Kanto

Each city has a test leader, like a gym leader, but the test is not always only a battle.

Kiunin's goal is still Class S fame, but now that goal starts to feel hollow.

## Saga 10: Skibidy Zone

Celadon sewers become corrupted.

Skibidy Toilet is tied to spatial corruption. Rooms connect wrong. Doors loop. People forget why they entered.

Skibidy Toilet is legendary/story-tier and does not use normal evolution.

Dante wants to use Skibidy energy to prepare cities for Tung Tung God's return.

Roan stops the full summoning but sees a vision of Luna inside the Sahur Realm.

Kiunin sees the vision too, but the lock attacks him immediately.

He gets angry, panics, and calls Roan a liar.

Then he quietly admits he saw something.

## Saga 11: License B

Roan struggles in real trainer battles.

His Brainrots do not fully trust him because he still hates them.

Kiunin tells him the truth in the worst way possible: Roan does not need to love Brainrots, but he cannot keep treating every one of them like Tung Tung God.

Roan starts to understand that many Brainrots are also victims of the incident.

Kiunin starts to understand that fame will not save him from being fake.

## Saga 12: Strawberry Helefanto

Roan and Kiunin reach the Red Garden.

Strawberry Helefanto is one of Tung Tung's great guards.

It was once tied to memory protection, but Tung Tung corrupted it and made it guard stolen memories.

It attacks grief, nostalgia, and the desire to forget pain.

Strawberry Helefanto uses stolen memories against Kiunin:

- his father praising him
- Class S fame
- tournament lights
- cameras
- a normal world where nothing hurts

Kiunin almost chooses the comfortable lie.

Roan reminds him that forgetting is not peace.

Dr. Renan Veyr sacrifices himself to protect Roan and Kiunin from a memory attack.

His last words to Kiunin are:

"I thought lies could protect you. I was wrong. Live in the truth, Kiunin."

Kiunin breaks.

For the first time, he remembers the truth and does not lose it.

He chooses the painful truth over the comfortable lie.

From this point on, Kiunin becomes Roan's real ally.

## Saga 13: Organization War

Roan and Kiunin learn that many groups fight over Brainrot power.

- Cognia Institute: hides truth to keep order.
- Cult of the First Sound: worships Tung Tung God.
- Grey Market: sells Brainrots and cognitive data.
- Skibidy Cult: wants reality corruption.
- 67 Hunters: want to weaponize 67.
- Red Garden: contains stolen emotional memories.
- Dante Vilar: manipulates all sides.

Kiunin no longer fights only for fame.

He still wants to be strong, but now he wants to prove someone born inside the lie can still choose truth.

## Saga 14: License A

Roan and Kiunin enter Area Null, a forbidden zone where reality bugs.

Names change. Paths repeat. NPCs speak out of order. The world behaves wrong.

Dante uses Area Null to test cognitive extraction machines.

Roan destroys one, but Dante gathers enough energy for the Pre-Awakening.

The sky darkens for a moment.

Everyone hears: Tung.

Kiunin hears it too and stays standing.

## Saga 15: True 67

The 67 Hunters try to capture 67.

67 awakens and traps people in meaningless gesture loops.

Roan and Kiunin defeat 67 but do not destroy it. They need 67 to open a controlled path to the Sahur Realm later.

Roan receives the 67 Signal.

Kiunin admits that numbers, sounds, and gestures can be prisons.

## Saga 16: License S

Roan returns to the ruins of the Central Hospital of Saffron.

The final test is mental. He must resist false memories, guilt, fear, and the temptation to forget Luna.

Kiunin enters a parallel mental test where he is offered the perfect fake life:

- his father alive
- Class S fame
- everyone cheering
- no Cognia lies
- no painful truth

Kiunin rejects it.

He says:

"If the world is fake, then I will tear the fake part down."

Roan receives License S.

Kiunin does not receive the same license yet, but earns something more important: his own truth.

## Saga 17: The Sahur Core

Dante opens the core below the hospital using four traces:

- Sahur Fragment
- 67 Signal
- Red Memory
- Skibidy Corruption

Dante reveals his plan: reduce Kanto's intelligence until nobody can resist Tung Tung God.

Roan and Kiunin defeat Dante, but the portal still opens.

## Saga 18: Sahur Realm

Roan and Kiunin enter the Sahur Realm.

It is a world made of stolen thoughts, hospital memories, wooden sounds, and repeated phrases.

Roan finds Luna, now 16 years old. She has been holding Tung Tung God back for ten years.

She warns Roan that destroying Tung Tung God carelessly could break all Brainrots and erase stolen memories forever.

Luna recognizes Kiunin as someone who broke a lock without being born with Roan's mutation.

This matters.

It proves people damaged by Sahur Noise can still choose truth.

## Saga 19: Four Altars

Roan, Luna, and Kiunin must shut down four altars before reaching the final god.

- Sound Altar
- Chaos Altar
- Corruption Altar
- Memory Altar

Each altar is a dungeon and reveals a part of the truth.

Brainrots are not demons. They are new life born from a disaster.

Kiunin guards Roan during memory attacks because Roan carries the strongest truth, but Kiunin now carries the strongest refusal to fall.

## Saga 20: Tung Tung God Form

Roan, Luna, and Kiunin reach the center of the Sahur Realm.

Dante appears one last time, almost empty, devoured by the god he served.

Tung Tung God absorbs Dante and awakens fully.

The first phase begins: Tung Tung God Form.

Tung Tung God is legendary and does not evolve like a normal Brainrot.

Its Supreme state is a final boss transformation, not a normal evolution.

It attacks memory, identity, and thought itself.

After being defeated, it absorbs the stored cognitive energy of Kanto and becomes Supreme Tung Tung.

## Final Battle: Supreme Tung Tung

Supreme Tung Tung is the true final boss.

It uses all the IQ stolen from Kanto and tries to erase Roan's mind completely.

Roan almost forgets Luna, his parents, his goal, and even his own name.

Luna helps him remember.

Kiunin keeps him standing.

Roan wins because he finally fights with his Brainrots instead of using them like tools.

Kiunin wins because he refuses to return to the lie.

When Supreme Tung Tung falls, its divine shell breaks.

Inside is a small wooden creature that repeats sounds because it never learned another way to exist.

## True ending

Roan chooses to destroy the god form, not the small original Tung Tung.

Luna returns to Kanto.

Memories begin to come back.

Kanto's average intelligence slowly recovers.

Brainrots keep existing, but they are no longer controlled by the god form.

Roan does not suddenly love all Brainrots. He still fears them sometimes. But now he understands that the real enemy was not every Brainrot. It was the theft of thought, memory, and choice.

Kiunin does not become calm or soft.

He stays intense, competitive, and emotional.

But now his courage has a direction.

He becomes proof that someone raised inside a lie can still choose truth.

## Post-game

After the ending, new areas open:

- Full Cave 67
- Deep Skibidy Sewers
- Red Garden Depths
- Brainrot Islands
- Supreme License Tournament
- Investigation of other regions affected by cognitive fall

## Core duo theme

Roan is the mind that remembers.

Kiunin is the heart that refuses to fall after remembering.

Roan exposes the lie.

Kiunin proves the lie can be escaped.
