# COGNIA Mental Test System

This document locks the intended lore for COGNIA tests and Tung Tung's hidden influence.

## Surface belief

In normal society, especially among lower-class members of Kanto, Brainrots are mostly seen as normal creatures.

They are treated like strange animals, dangerous sometimes, useful sometimes, but not supernatural.

This is important because COGNIA's public image depends on that belief. If most people think Brainrots are just creatures, then COGNIA can run experiments without causing panic.

## What COGNIA thinks it is doing

COGNIA believes it created a mental-space testing system.

The official explanation is:

- The user's mind becomes a controlled test area.
- The system measures focus, fear, memory, and obedience.
- The candidate sees symbolic rooms, corridors, doors, and false signals.
- Passing the test proves the candidate can handle Brainrot pressure.

COGNIA does not fully understand the system. They think they understand enough, because the results look useful.

## What is actually happening

The mental-space tests are one of Tung Tung Sahur's slow methods for entering human minds.

Tung Tung uses the test system to:

- explore the user's mind
- identify people with high IQ or strong memory resistance
- mark priority targets
- learn which thoughts are easiest to weaken
- prepare future IQ theft

It is slow and subtle because Tung Tung is not as naturally skilled at mental control as 67.

## The disguised scientist

The cultist of Tung Tung Sahur is secretly a high-ranking COGNIA scientist.

He hides inside the foundation and pretends the mental-space system is just advanced science.

His role:

- expand COGNIA's use of mind tests
- make researchers overconfident
- hide Tung Tung's influence inside official procedures
- increase the number of users exposed to mental-space scans
- help Tung Tung search for high-value minds

## Why COGNIA accepts it

Tung Tung cannot control COGNIA directly the way 67 can affect minds.

Instead, Tung Tung helps the scientist push ideas that feel reasonable.

COGNIA members slowly start believing:

- the system is safe
- they understand enough
- the strange effects are normal brain stress
- failures are just weak candidates
- memory gaps are acceptable test side effects

This lets the system spread without anyone realizing it is a trap.

## 67's role

67 is not exactly Tung Tung's ally.

67 is powerful enough to resist being used as a normal pawn.

Tung Tung wants to use 67's stronger control ability, but cannot fully command it.

That creates tension:

- Tung Tung wants IQ and priority targets.
- 67 wants freedom and influence.
- COGNIA thinks both are only data problems.
- Roan slowly realizes they are dealing with separate threats, not one simple villain team.

## Gameplay direction

COGNIA tests should not feel like normal quizzes.

They should feel like mental spaces:

- hallways
- fake safe rooms
- doors with misleading comfort
- NPC voices that sound normal but are wrong
- memories with small contradictions
- paths that reward resisting the crowd
- rooms where Brainrots look harmless but the signal feels wrong

Good test design:

- Two choices can both look believable.
- The obvious answer should sometimes be bait.
- The player should learn the lore through pressure, not lecture.
- The system should feel official at first and creepy later.

## Early example: Brock License D

Brock's test is not a quiz.

It is a small COGNIA mental maze.

The player sees a hallway in their mind. One path shows the common safe belief: Brainrots are normal creatures. Another path is silent, weird, and less comfortable. Passing means noticing that the safe public story is not the whole truth.

Brock does not fully understand the system either. He uses it because License D requires it, but he is uneasy about what it does.
